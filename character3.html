Backstory

Born into a tightly bound nomadic caravan family that traveled the roads of the realm, the elven woman grew up under traditions that were less about freedom and more about obligation. Within her clan, individual choice was secondary to duty: she was expected to marry within the group to preserve lineage and loyalty. As she came of age, the only prospects offered to her were deeply unsettling—one was a human man obsessed with the idea of fathering half-elven children, treating her more as a vessel for legacy than a partner, and the other a broad-shouldered, volatile enforcer whose reputation for violence made her wary of ever being alone with him. The sense of confinement within these expectations hardened over time, until compliance felt like a slow erasure of self.

Unable to accept that future, she fled under cover of night, leaving behind the only life she had ever known and the expectations that came with it. Her journey eventually led her to Lyrabar, where she sought anonymity and work among strangers, relying on her talents rather than her lineage. Her skill in performance and grace quickly drew attention, earning her an invitation to entertain at the king’s ball—a chance to reinvent herself in glittering halls far removed from dusty caravan roads. Yet her disappearance did not go unnoticed; at least two men from her former life were deeply displeased by her absence—one seeing her escape as a personal slight and lost opportunity, the other as an insult to the authority he believed she owed him. Their dissatisfaction lingers like a distant storm, even as she steps into a life she can finally claim as her own.
