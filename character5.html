Backstory

Vichenzo Visconti was born into privilege, but never quite felt comfortable with it. As the son of Chancellor Valis Visconti, he spent his childhood within the Citadel of the Stalwart King, growing up among nobles, advisors, and royal servants. He was a close companion was Prince Edric, and together the boys explored the halls of the palace, listened to tales of King Alric's adventures, and imagined the great deeds they would one day accomplish themselves. Yet while Edric dreamed of glory and heroism, Vichenzo found himself captivated by mysteries. He was the sort of child who cared less about who won a battle than why it had been fought, less interested in legendary treasures than in who had made them and what secrets they contained. Though his father hoped he might one day enter royal service, it soon became apparent that Vichenzo's path would be a different one.

When he reached adolescence, strange manifestations began to follow him. Books opened to pages he had been seeking. Candle flames changed color when his emotions ran high. Once, during a heated argument with a tutor, a burst of uncontrolled arcane energy shattered every window in the study alcove. The court's sages quickly recognized the signs of innate sorcery. Concerned that such power might become dangerous without guidance, Valis sought the finest teacher in Impiltur and found one in Ultheera Windmere. Leaving the court was difficult. For the first time in his life, Vichenzo was separated from both his father and Prince Edric. Yet beneath Ultheera's instruction at the Tower of the Wind, he flourished. She taught him discipline, caution, and respect for powers older than kingdoms. More importantly, she taught him that knowledge was a responsibility, not merely a pursuit.

Vichenzo became one of Ultheera's most promising students. While other apprentices focused on mastering increasingly powerful spells, he developed a fascination with ancient magical structures, forgotten civilizations, and the lingering enchantments hidden beneath the modern world. He spent countless nights studying obscure records and fragmented histories, searching for connections others overlooked. When the renowned sorceress announced that she intended to investigate the chasm personally, she chose Vichenzo to accompany her. The journey would be Vichenzo's first true expedition beyond the safety of libraries, classrooms, and royal halls. He departed with excitement, confidence, and a scholar's hunger for discovery.
