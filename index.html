1502 DR - The Year of the Seven Calamities

Woe betide the realm upon which fate has cast its judgment.

Ancient powers stir... world—forces long believed dormant, each awakening more destructive than the last. Omen-seekers and arcanists point to a chilling prophecy recorded centuries ago by Augathra the Mad, the enigmatic “Lost Sage” of Candlekeep. Within the vast archives of Candlekeep, she inscribed thousands of calendrical year-names, each one intended as a portent of future events. Remarkably, many of these predictions have proven true, aligning uncannily with the rise and fall of kingdoms across Faerûn.

Now, one such name has come to pass: 1502 - The Year of the Seven Calamities. Across Faerûn, sages, diviners, and self-proclaimed prophets speak in hushed tones of what this designation might mean. Some believe it marks seven sequential world-shaking disasters. Others argue it is symbolic—a fractured truth pointing to a single, world-ending event viewed through seven lenses of suffering. Regardless of interpretation, one certainty remains: the fabric of fate feels strained, as though reality itself is bracing for impact.

The only question that matters now is this: which corner of the world will suffer first? Will it be a forgotten kingdom swallowed by disaster, or a thriving land brought low in an instant? And more importantly—when the calamities begin, will heroes rise to resist them, or will the world itself fracture under their weight?
